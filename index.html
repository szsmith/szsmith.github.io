I work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, financial services, and energy, with research focused on AI governance, IT audit, and emerging-technology risk. My work bridges deeply technical systems and the institutions that have to deploy, audit, and regulate them.

Before joining the University of Kentucky, I spent two decades in industry and federal research, including roles at Microsoft, Amazon Web Services, AT&T, PwC, and Bank of America, with research engagements through NSF and NIST. That combined vantage point of practitioner, auditor, and academic now informs how I think, write, and host the Professor Z Podcast.

03 · Research & Writing

Areas of research

My peer-reviewed research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and the regulated industries that have to deploy these systems. A complete publication list is maintained on Google Scholar.

Adversarial AI & LLM Red-Teaming

Research on the transferability of adversarial attacks across large language models, with emphasis on attacks generated on open-weight surrogate models transferring to closed-weight blackbox targets.

AI Governance for Critical Infrastructure

How AI systems deployed in energy, financial services, healthcare, and other regulated industries create governance gaps that existing audit and compliance frameworks were not designed to address.

Federal AI Compliance

NIST Risk Management Framework, FedRAMP, and Authority to Operate processes applied to AI systems. Direct expert input to NIST on the AI Risk Management Framework.

Energy Sector Cybersecurity

NERC CIP compliance, OT/IT convergence in utilities, and the regulatory and policy questions emerging at the intersection of renewables, distributed assets, and grid security.
